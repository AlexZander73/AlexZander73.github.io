Short signals worth keeping

Longer writing belongs on the blog. This page is for field notes, recurring themes, and small observations that shape the work before they turn into polished posts.

Field Notes

Mar 17, 2026

Offline-first earns trust

Users may never say "thanks for graceful degradation," but they absolutely feel the absence of it. For site work, travel, and reference tools, reliable local behavior is part of the brand.

Mar 17, 2026

Calm interfaces are faster

Premium UI is less about decoration and more about decision pressure. Less noise, more rhythm, and fewer dead-end states usually outperform flashy complexity.

Mar 17, 2026

Local AI works best on narrow jobs

The strongest use cases stay small: summarise this note set, pull likely context, draft a compact first pass. The moment the scope gets fuzzy, the value often drops.

Mar 17, 2026

Small systems scale better than big promises

A good static page, a readable workflow, and a few dependable files can do more long-term work than an ambitious stack nobody wants to touch six months later.

Reading Lines

  • Local-first AI. I keep circling back to private workflows, narrow evaluation, and the real cost of model complexity.
  • Interface craft. Typography, rhythm, motion, and the small decisions that make software feel composed instead of improvised.
  • Low-maintenance systems. Static publishing, sane deployment paths, and tools that do not turn their own upkeep into a job.
  • Travel and mapping. The overlap between route memory, place, movement, and how to make notes from the road actually usable later.

Route Archive

  • Work loops. Short urban walks used to think through stuck product decisions, especially structure and hierarchy problems.
  • Coastal resets. Routes that simplify thinking and usually lead to cutting scope rather than adding more features.
  • Tool scouting. Hardware stores, signage systems, maps, and public interfaces that end up informing how products explain themselves.
