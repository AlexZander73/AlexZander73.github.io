Case Studies

Carpentry Companion (AU)

Problem: Give an offline-first carpentry app a public product surface that satisfies support and App Store policy needs.

Constraint: Keep it static, clear, and easy to update without another service.

Outcome: Product, support, privacy, and catalog pages now live in a structure that can scale to more apps.

Main site rebuild

Problem: Turn a clean-but-thin personal site into something with proof, personality, and visible momentum.

Constraint: GitHub Pages only, no frameworks, graceful external integrations.

Outcome: The site now carries projects, products, notes, a lab, a uses page, and richer visual structure without losing simplicity.

Local AI experiments

Problem: Make local-model workflows useful instead of impressive-looking.

Constraint: Private by default, small scope, easily checked outputs.

Outcome: The work is converging around narrow note, retrieval, and decision-support loops rather than broad assistant theater.

Questions I'm Testing

  • What parts of AI-assisted workflows are actually worth keeping local, and which parts are just novelty?
  • How simple can a product-support stack stay before it stops being trustworthy?
  • Can a travel notes system remain useful if capture is nearly frictionless and structure only appears later?
  • How much motion makes a site feel premium before it starts costing clarity?
